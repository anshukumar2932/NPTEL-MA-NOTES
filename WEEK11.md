# 🧠 PART 1: TEXT MINING BASICS

---

### ❓ Q1. What is text mining?

👉 **Answer:**
Process of converting **unstructured text into structured data** for analysis.

---

### ❓ Q2. What is unstructured data?

👉 **Answer:**
Data without a fixed format (e.g., reviews, emails, tweets)

---

### ❓ Q3. What is structured data?

👉 **Answer:**
Data in tabular form (rows & columns)

---

### ❓ Q4. What is a corpus?

👉 **Answer:**
👉 A **collection of text documents**

---

### ❓ Q5. What are common sources of text mining data?

👉 **Answer:**

* Emails
* Blogs
* Reviews
* Social media
* HR data

# 🔴 PART 2: TEXT PREPROCESSING

---

### ❓ Q6. What is preprocessing in text mining?

👉 **Answer:**
Cleaning text before analysis

---

### ❓ Q7. Steps in preprocessing?

👉 **Answer:**

1. Remove stopwords
2. Remove punctuation
3. Tokenization
4. Stemming (optional)

---

### ❓ Q8. What is tokenization?

👉 **Answer:**
👉 Breaking text into words

---

### ❓ Q9. What are stopwords?

👉 **Answer:**
👉 Common words (e.g., “the”, “is”) that add no meaning

---

### ❓ Q10. What is stemming?

👉 **Answer:**
👉 Reducing words to root form
(e.g., running → run)

---

# 🔴 PART 3: TEXT REPRESENTATION

---

### ❓ Q11. What is TF-IDF?

👉 **Answer:**
👉 Measures importance of a word in a document

---

### ❓ Q12. High TF-IDF means?

👉 **Answer:**
👉 Word is important in that document

---

### ❓ Q13. Why not use simple frequency?

👉 **Answer:**
👉 Common words appear everywhere → not useful

$$

$$
